Understanding Hydration Ratios in Sourdough

A field guide to sourdough hydration ratios, from lean loaves to enriched brioche.

Nora Fields

2 March 2026 7 min read

Hydration ratio describes the amount of water relative to flour by weight, and it is the single biggest variable in how a dough behaves.

What is hydration, exactly

A dough at 100g flour and 70g water sits at 70% hydration. Most rustic country loaves fall between 65% and 80%.

Lean doughs: 60–70%

Baguettes and sandwich loaves usually sit at the lower end, which keeps the dough easier to shape by hand.

High hydration: 75–85%

Open-crumb country loaves and ciabatta live here. Expect a slack, sticky dough that rewards a light touch during folds.

Every ten percent of added water changes the dough more than any other single variable in the recipe.

Enriched doughs: 50–60%

Brioche and milk bread run drier once you account for butter and eggs, which behave like additional liquid in the mix.

  • Lean rustic loaf: 70% hydration
  • Ciabatta: 80% hydration
  • Brioche: 55% hydration plus enrichments

Start conservative and add water in small increments until the dough matches the description for your target style.
