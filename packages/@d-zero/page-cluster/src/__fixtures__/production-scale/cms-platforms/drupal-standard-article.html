Getting Started with Home Composting

Submitted by Alex Rivera on June 18, 2026

Composting at home has become one of the most accessible ways for city dwellers to reduce food waste while producing nutrient-rich soil for potted plants and community gardens.

Getting started requires little more than a small bin, some dry leaves or shredded paper, and a consistent habit of turning the pile every week or two.

Within a few months, most households find that a well-maintained compost system dramatically cuts down on the volume of trash sent to the curb.

Local municipalities increasingly offer subsidized bins and drop-off programs, making the barrier to entry lower than ever before.
