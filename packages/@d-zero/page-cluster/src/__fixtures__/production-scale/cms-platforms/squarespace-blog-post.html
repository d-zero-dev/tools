Why We Changed Our House Blend

April 14, 2026
Mika Tanaka
12 Comments

Every March we run a full cupping review of the house blend. This year, three of our five reviewers flagged the finish as flat compared to last season's batches.

What changed in the cup

The Ethiopian lot that anchored last year's blend came in lower on the cupping table this harvest, so we rebalanced the ratio toward a washed Colombian.

Adjusting the roast curve

We pulled first crack forward by about twenty seconds and extended the development phase slightly to compensate for the new lot's density.

  • Charge temperature: 200°C
  • First crack: 8:45
  • Drop: 11:20
The new blend reads sweeter up front and cleaner on the finish — exactly what we were chasing.

Bags with the updated blend started shipping this week. Let us know what you taste.
